Summarize the changes to sedition laws under the BNS.

Under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023, the specific offense of "Sedition" (previously Section 124A of the IPC) has been removed. It is replaced by Section 152, which deals with "Acts endangering sovereignty, unity and integrity of India".

Statutory Reference BNS Sec. 152
"Whoever, purposely or knowingly, by words... excites secession or armed rebellion or subversive activities, or encourages feelings of separatist activities... shall be punished with imprisonment for life or with imprisonment which may extend to seven years..."

Key Differences:

  • Terminology: The term "Rajdroh" (treason against government) is replaced by "Deshdroh" (treason against nation).
  • Scope: The focus shifts from "disaffection towards the Government" to acts threatening the "sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India."
  • Punishment: The gap in sentencing under IPC (3 years or Life) has been bridged. BNS prescribes up to 7 years or Life Imprisonment.
